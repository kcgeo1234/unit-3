GDP is an important indicator of the economic health of a nation or a region and is used to track changes in economic activity over time. Then, what is the difference between GDP and personal income? Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is a measure of the total value of "goods and services" produced within a region during a specific period of time while the personal income only means the amount of money an individual receives from all sources during the time.
